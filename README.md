        <h2 style="opacity:0.9;">HOW TO MAKE AN ARL-44</h2>
        <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/ARL-44.jpg/250px-ARL-44.jpg" width="710" style="opacity:0.9;">
        <div class="recipe" style="opacity:0.9;">
          <p>This recipe has been forgotten for 60 years, but we at The tank chef have recovered it. The ARL-44 is a french heavy tank cooked up in "l'atelier de construction de rueil." When it was first developped it used a lot of rare ingredients like ship parts; fortunatly we have adapted the recipe for casual bakers.</p>
          <p>Here's what you'll need:</p>
          <ul>
            <li>Steel(for the original recipe use the scrap metal from the battlecruiser Dunkerque for the turret)</li>
            <li>A 90mm SA45 cannon</li>
            <li>Two 7.5 MAC 31 machine guns</li>
            <li>A Maybach HL 230 V12 gasoline engine</li>
            <li>(Optional) Tools like shovels,axes,picaxes,etc.</li>
          </ul>
          <p>Once you've gathered all the ingredients take out your cutting board:</p>
          <ol>
            <li>Cut the steel into the shape of the hull of the ARL-44 and a rectangular prism then put them in a bowl and mix well.</li>
            <li>Add the V12 engine, 90mm cannon and 2 MAC 10 machine guns then keep mixing</li>
            <li>Put it in the microwave for 3 minutes sprinkles the tools and enjoy!</li>
          </ol>
          <img src="https://static.encyclopedia.warthunder.com/images/fr_battleship_dunkerque.png" width="600">
          <p>If you are curious, the Dunkerque was a French battlecruiser built during the interwar period. After the fall of France it was scuttled to prevent it from being captured by the Germans.</p>
